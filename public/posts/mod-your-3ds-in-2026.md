The 3DS eShop closed in March 2023. Online multiplayer will eventually follow. Nintendo has made clear they're done with the platform. If you still have a 3DS — or can find one for $60 on eBay — modding it is the single best thing you can do for the device.

Here's the pitch.

## Why Mod a 3DS in 2026?

Because the 3DS library is extraordinary and Nintendo has left you no legal way to buy most of it.

The eShop is closed. That means every game that was digital-only is now inaccessible through official channels. Every game that had a small print run and is now $80+ physical — also inaccessible unless you pay scalper prices. The Virtual Console library, which included Game Boy, Game Boy Color, Game Boy Advance, NES, and SNES games, is gone.

Modding your 3DS gives you:

- Access to your own backed-up cartridge library without the cartridge
- The ability to run games from regions your console doesn't officially support (the Japanese library has games that never came west)
- Game Boy, GBA, NES, SNES emulation running natively on the hardware
- DS game compatibility in native DS mode
- Custom themes, homebrew applications, and tools
- Complete ownership of your device

The 3DS eShop is dead. The StreetPass Relay has been shut down. The 3DS's commercial life is over. Modding it is not piracy advocacy — it's the only realistic path to preserving and expanding what the hardware can do.

## Which 3DS Should You Get?

If you don't already own one, here's the hierarchy:

**New 3DS XL** — The best option. Larger screens, the extra C-stick nub and bumpers, faster processor (matters for some homebrew). Found for $80-120 in decent condition.

**New 3DS (small)** — Same internals as the XL, smaller form factor. Personal preference.

**Old 3DS XL** — Fine. Misses the extra buttons and speed but plays everything and is cheaper.

**2DS / New 2DS XL** — Budget options. The New 2DS XL is actually a good device at a low price. No 3D effect, which some people prefer.

The "New" prefix matters — New 3DS and New 2DS have a faster CPU, more RAM, and the C-stick. Some homebrew applications require it.

Japanese models are region-locked but CFW bypasses this entirely — same advice as the DSi LL. Import to save money.

## How to Mod It

The current method uses **Luma3DS** (custom firmware) installed via **3ds.hacks.guide**, the definitive guide maintained by the homebrew community. The process varies by system version but the guide handles every case.

At a high level:

1. **Check your firmware version.** Some older firmwares have different exploits.
2. **Run the exploit.** The current main method uses a seedminer-based approach that works on any unmodded 3DS regardless of firmware, using a DSiWare exploit.
3. **Install boot9strap.** This is the low-level bootloader that gives you permanent CFW.
4. **Install Luma3DS.** This is the CFW layer that enables homebrew, patches region locking, and adds features.

After that, you install **FBI** (title manager), **Checkpoint** (save manager), and whatever else you want. The whole process takes 1-3 hours and is thoroughly documented.

**3ds.hacks.guide** is the only guide you need. Don't use YouTube tutorials — they go out of date. The written guide is maintained and current.

## What to Install

After CFW, the useful software:

**Twilight Menu++** — plays DS games from your SD card in native DS mode. Better than DS mode on any flashcard.

**mGBA** — GBA emulator. Plays GBA games better than the 3DS's own Virtual Console implementation (which used an emulator anyway, not native hardware).

**RetroArch** — multi-system emulator frontend. NES, SNES, Game Boy, Game Boy Color, all running well.

**JKSM / Checkpoint** — save backups. Back up every save before it's gone.

**Universal-Updater** — homebrew app store that keeps everything updated.

## The Game Library

The 3DS library has around 1,300 games. Highlights:

- The Pokémon games (X/Y, ORAS, Sun/Moon, Ultra) natively
- Fire Emblem Awakening and Fates — two of the best in the series
- The Legend of Zelda: A Link Between Worlds and Majora's Mask 3D
- Shin Megami Tensei IV and IV: Apocalypse
- Monster Hunter 3 Ultimate and 4 Ultimate
- Rune Factory 4
- Etrian Odyssey IV, Untold I and II, V
- Bravely Default and Bravely Second
- Luigi's Mansion: Dark Moon
- Kid Icarus: Uprising

Plus the full DS library through TWiLight Menu++, the GBA library through mGBA, and the Game Boy and GBC library through RetroArch.

This is an enormous amount of software on a device that fits in your pocket, costs $60-100 used, and will run all of it from a single SD card.

## Do It

The 3DS is at the end of its commercial life. Nintendo has made their money on it and moved on. The homebrew and CFW community will keep it alive indefinitely — same as they've done for the GBA, DS, Wii, and PSP before it.

Mod your 3DS. Back up your saves. Expand what the hardware can do. You own the device — you might as well own it completely.
